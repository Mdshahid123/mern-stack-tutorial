Holi

Introduction

Holi is one of the most popular festivals of India. It is also known as the "Festival of Colors". People of all ages celebrate it with great joy and enthusiasm.

Why We Celebrate Holi

Holi is celebrated to mark the victory of good over evil. It is linked to the story of Prahlad and Holika from Hindu mythology. The burning of Holika is a symbol of destroying evil

How Holi is Celebrated

Playing with Colors

On Holi, people throw dry colors and splash colored water on each other. Children use water balloons and pichkaris to play joyfully.

Traditional Foods

Special sweets and drinks are prepared during Holi. People enjoy gujiya, malpua, mathri, and thandai with friends and family.

songs and Dance

Groups of people gather to sing folk songs and dance. This creates a festive and joyful environment everywhere.

meetings and Relatives /h3>

People visit their neighbors, relatives, and friends to exchange greetings and sweets. Holi strengthens bonds of love and unity.

conclustion

Holi is not just a festival of colors but also a festival of togetherness. It spreads happiness, unity, and peace among people.
